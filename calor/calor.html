CALOR

Digital Faucet System

Efficient and reliable shower experience

Calor is a faucet system that safely and efficiently delivers your desired water temperature with a temperature sensor and promotes a mindful water use with integration technology. It’s smart to let you set your water temperature and suggest when to get out of shower.

Unpredictable Adjustment

Adjusting for 2.7 times in average causes unnecessary time, effort and water waste

Chance Of Burning

There are over 3000 scalding cases each year and over 90% incidents occur at home

Underestimated Water Usage

A Growing Concern

More than 34 states are expecting water shortage in the next few years

In respond to recent articles and news on California drought crisis, saving water becomes more important and inevitable.

A Viable Solution

Current market offers faucet fixtures/system with either simple yet limited functions at a low cost or high-end at a cost up to $5000, not including cost for installing or any pipe rerouting.

Disassemblying a fixure and understand how a valve and a cartridge work together, along with the conclusion from the maket analysis matrix, result an approach: innovate through improving existing experience.

As cartridge remains to be a hardware, I begin to explore organic/dynamic forms that would give a break from the traditional handle-turning faucet fixture sterotype.

Besides a physical form, an interface would provide feedbacks to users as they set the temperature for instance, so I quickly explore the information representation as well as the interaction.

Through series refining, I clean up the user interface to a level that sufficiently and quickly tells information yet not overwhelms with complexity. I keep the “turning-a-nob” interaction because it connects the circular interface and reminds nostalgic and analogic interaction of turning the handle of a faucet.

Designing from outside in, I then study the structure and mechanism of existing models and try to devise how parts move in order to deliver the interaction I intend with.

With this attempt, I harness the power of a motor and perform the same task with a 3D-printed prototype.

I prototype the setting temperature interaction with an arduino to test the setting and counting down. Check out the code on Github.

Connectivity in the system

According to a survey I conduct, 1/3 of people have their phone with them while they wait for the water to get hot. Hence I could harness this result with the system response diagram I made at the begining, and make the phone more useful by aggregating data and informing suggestions to users.

Extracting functions from the diagram, I lay out the overall architecture for this mobile app and then wireframe focusing on the layout of elements and interaction.

Transitioning from wireframes to mid-fidelity mockup still requires some thoughts into put elements in the a better order for clearer information hierarchy.
