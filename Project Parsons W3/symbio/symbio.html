Symbio
Next way to experience sound

Symbio is network of devices that changes the way you experience sound. Integrating hardware and software, Symbio not only helps people with progressive hearing loss, but also the enhances music listening experience, serves as a baby monitor and expands beyond.

Warren

  • 30-yr old male with progressive hearing loss
  • Uses bilateral hearing aids
  • Struggles with face-to-face communication
  • Finds Siri doesn't understand his voice
  • Feels increasingly isolated

General Population

  • 58% of American adults have a smart phone
  • 52% of smart phone users listen to music with their phone

3 types of hearing loss


Sensorineural:Hearing loss due to damage to nerve damage in the inner ear

Conductive:Hearing loss due to damage to ear canal, ear drum, or middle ear

Mixed:Hearing loss due to combination of sensorineural and conductive hearing loss

Proper hearing aid is often a good solution to hearing loss, but choose one can sometimes be daunting

- Hearing Loss Association of America

Looking at the market, We noticed the potential of better communication implementation into hearing devices with nowadays technology.

We need one fix-all device that changes the way we experience sound

To ensure smaller dimension, healthier sound transmission and less stigma, we decide to implement bluetooth, bone conduction and gel to stick the device on the back of the ear.

To test out the viability of bone conduction, we prototype with audio cord and piezo buzzer elements as a proof of concept.

As we experiment with bone conduction, we discover another spot on the back of the head that allows effective sound transmission.

Thus, we take advantage of this and expand the traditional headphone experience, giving the third sound source for sports and active use.

Finalizing the form of both devices with 3D printing and female molding.

A microphone enables audio filtering for one with hearing loss and also serves as a convenient remote mic for general population daily use.

One that ties them both - an App

We list potential functions and sort out the information architecture.

It takes a series of thinking and planning to solve the logic, relations within functions and their corresponding wireframes.

We develop an interface style guide for the app to create a consistent and cohesive layout throughout the use of the application.

Technology drives viability

Along with the software, the chosen hardware and technology implementation ensure the pausibility and usability of our product.
