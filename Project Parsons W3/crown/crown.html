Crown
Origami Cardboard Stool

Crown is handcrafted corrugated cardboard stool that harnesses the nature of origami. Crown inherits the beauty and power from folding, resulting both a royal but not overdecorated pattern, and a strong but not overused structure.

The challenge of this project is to create a chair out of a 4' x 8' single sheet of corrugated cardboard, which can support 200lbs, with maximum cut into 3 pieces and no glue or fasteners.

Doing research on the past projects, pattern, geometry, origami and cardboard, I understand the property and possibility of the material and gain inspirations from the relationship between papaer and folding.

I first generate concepts from two directions: form and function; then seek a way to sythesize them.

However, thinking on paper does not always help visualize the possibility of forms. As a result, I start folding bond paper, bristol board and Tiziano paper because I have something in mind:

How can I harmonize origami and cardboard in a chair?

After hours of folding, I reach a pattern that can support and lock itself.

Some more detail sketches after visualizing with models.

To conduct a compression testing, I go to the gym and put 3 of 50-lb weight on the the stool, which holds perfectly still. With me sitting on it (over 300lbs in total), the stool supports as well as before.
