Native
New Parking Experience

Native is a redesign of parking system and kiosk UI. The synthesis of both results from taking consideration of user experience and interaction, to deliver an easier and more efficient way to park and pay.

On-site research on issues users currently encounter in parking and paying.

Not just the UI, but the system

I realize the issues are about not only the UI, but the entire system.
Thus, a better parking system is in demand to solve all the problems.

Pay by spot ? Instead, pay by zone

In the new parking system, I divide all spots into 10 zones; in each zone there is one pole, which plays the significant role in memory offloading.

Harness Existing Technology

Recognition rather than recall

Using recognition instead of recall provides memoery offload, which benefits users a more effortless parking payment experience.

You can forget about the space number
but not how your car looks like

With a series of iteration on UI and UX for feedbacks and testing, I reached the 10th iteration as my final design.

I learn the basics Objective-C and Cocoa Touch to build a prototype of the UI on iPad, for the purpose of demo during project presentation.
