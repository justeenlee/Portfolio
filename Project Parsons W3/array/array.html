Parsons
Graphic Design Series

Parsons is a collection of graphic design work during Parsons Summer Intensive Studies in 2014. This series contains various directions of graphic design, including typography, carving, pattern, stamp, poster, logo, branding, CD album, book, book jacket and photography/composition.

7 DEADLY SINS POSTAGE STAMPS

"You've probably committed most of them.
Now design a set of stamps to illustrate them"

Seeing sins through a lion

Instead of using human, I choose to use lion to represent the Seven Deadly Sins, because looking at sins through an animal causes people to think and reflect more about themselves.


In terms of medium, I only use pen and ink to drop black lines with gothic font to re-create the mood of the early 14th century.

ALBUM COVER

"Create an album cover for a fictitious band, using a fictitious album name and an image from Flickr"

JSmooth and something to be alive are the random name and title of the band, since I can draw a relationship in between the two to ensure harmony of the cover:

In computer science, JSmooth is a wrapper that compiles a .jar file into a .exe file, which makes a java file executable, as if making something alive.

After the digital work, I mount the print onto a piece of chipboard to give it some rigidity and weight, so it feels like a real album in hand.

POSTER FOR DESIGN LECTURE

"Design a poster for an exhibition and mimic the style of the designers in the design"

My assigned designer is Raymond Loewy, whose influential work includes the PRR S1 Locomotive, the Lucky Strike Cigarette packaging, the Coca Cola bottle, the Shell logo, the Nabisco logo and so many more.

I choose to mimic the design of the Lucky Strike packaging, since it's not only Loewy's iconic design, but also a brand most of us would recognize.

TITLE PAGES

"Design a title page for the following books:
Lolita, Naked Lunch & Frankenstein.

Sex, drugs & horror through typography

Lolita, Naked Lunch and Frankenstein are classic novels about sex, drugs and horror respectively. Thus, I attempt to design the titles pages to convey those themes solely through typography.

GIG POSTER

"A tradition of art posters for indie bands.
Design one for a gig in NYC this summer, June 20"

A San Francisco based indie band, THE SHE'S, consists of 4 girls and carries lively music. I choose this band because I used to go to highschool with one of the girls and she is still a good friend of mine.

This lock-up is the title of one of their albums, of which I emphasize the red color and "SUMMER" since I create this in early June.

I create this character to carry out the young and energetic feeling of the band, since they wear sunglasses and Converse high-top shoes and run around in some of their videos.

Branding

"Design a brand with a table tent, shopping bag, business card and another application"

After a couple of hours of ideation, I explore any possible direction I think of and generate 237 ideas for the logo, emphazing the letter "K".

The 3D glasses element takes a shift from the paragraphic text and leads you to the business card.

I combine the conventional business card with 3D glasses; besides playful experience, since they come in pairs, customers are likely take two business cards (if they lose one accidentally, they still have another one).

With 3D glasses, customers can see a 3D logo on the shopping bag.

To show exlusivity of VIPs, every membership card comes with a sleeve, which shows a "blinking" logo as the card slides out.

Book Design

"Design a book about getting lost in New York City"

I am intrigued by the fancy lights and billboards in Times Square, so I decide to bring that exciting yet slightly overwhelming experience through the book.

3. video of promotion

Arrows and color-coded numbers indicate the order to open the book, resembling the NYC subway signs.

This interaction and the map lead readers to read from the bottom.

As the beginning of Broadway, these is nothing much crazy going on, which explains why it's black and white at first.

Towards Times Sqaure, more lights and signs start to emerge, shown by the gradient transition from 43rd Street.

Reaching Times Square brings in all blasting and continuous billboards and advertisements, exciting walkers, tourists or everyone walking nearby.
