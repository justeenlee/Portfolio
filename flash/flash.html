Flash

Idea Collector & Incubator App

Where centralizes your ideas

Flash is an idea collector and incubator. It allows you to log your flashing ideas in a fast yet organized and fun way as well as smartly prompts you for further development of your ideas based on related events such as date and location.

All content will be updated by 2/1

Including:
paper prototype, framer prototype, user testing, A/B testing, app animation, app interaction, app detail and justifications

What's on the market?

To understand users’ behaviors and habits, the research begins with 5 semi-structured interviews with designers that have different backgrounds and years of experience across several companies.

Sorting data from 5 interviews with an affinity diagram, I gather insights about the types and contents of inputs designers have when they document their new ideas.

With the affinity diagram, I create two personas to represent different user groups with their own characteristics and help guide the design with the summarized research trends and patterns.

Is it possible to log and retrieve ideas in a faster yet organized and fun way?

Connect many dots

To speed up the process of documenting information of a new idea, I relate that to filling a form, which has predetermined fields, so that users could fill out an organized idea quickly.

Yet, although I account for the fact to mininize the numbers of fields and only leave the ones that users always fill out, the pattern is not consistent throughout testing.

Exploring different search methods, I have an idea of boolean search because of its accuracy of finding a specific idea by the combination of input. However, through A/B testing, I find users search what they can think of instead of holding fairly precise memory.

I don’t really go back and think what I put in before. Instead, I search whatever I can think of at the moment.

- Anonymous testing participant

I wish it's more active, like helping me develop my ideas.

- Anonymous testing participant

As I am smoothing out the user flow with the app, a new insight kicks in: to make it more active, which is a valid point and component as part of the idea collecting process.

Move from static to dynamic visualization

To test and demo all these interactions, I prototype them with Framer Studio for its flexibility and interactivity across different platforms. Check out the trailer video and some framer prototypes:

1. Insert to add | Swipe between the two
2. Quick Search | Swipe along the side
3. List to detail view | Double tap on an item

However, Too dull to be willing to interact

Yet, lacking visual sophistication in prototype 1 makes it too plain and pale and potentially drives less engagement within the app, so I decide to bring back the tangbile and playful "card feeling".

To enrich the visual elements, I experiment with more representation of a card on screen, varying from size, shape and color.

Insert to add

Swipe between cards

Have a new idea? Simply swipe between any two cards to bring in a new idea card and you are ready to edit.

Shred to delete

Hold and swipe

You don't often delete an idea, but it's fun when you do since you would shred it and it goes to a trash can that is scheduled to permanately deletes all items.

Suggestions

Drag and drop

Leverage machine learning to collect your own category titles and prompt you suggestions as you add new content to keep your ideas efficiently organized.

Flip for more

Double tap or swipe a little

Want to add more content or see more detail? Flip the card and get all you want to know.

Quick search

Swipe along the edge

Tired of scrolling through your 1000 ideas to find a speicfic one? Simply swipe along the edge of your phone to pull down the search menu.

Stamp a card

Tap the thumbs-up

Highlight all your remarkable ideas with a thumbs-up stamp at the corner and identify them easily all at once when searching/filtering.

API Intergration

Ease your workflow

Use Pinterest for inspiration? Now you can directly link your favourite pins to your idea.

Rediscover I

Cheer the location

Subtly encourage you to develop your idea as you return to the location where you came up with that idea.

Rediscover II

Cheer the date

Subtly encourage you to develop your idea when you are on the same date that you came up with that idea.
